SECTION A — Material Properties
f'c MPa
Concrete compressive strength (cylinder, 28 days)
f'c is measured from a standard cylinder (150×300 mm) crushed at 28 days. It is the most important concrete property. Higher f'c reduces the compression block depth: \(a = \frac{A_s f_y}{0.85 f'_c b}\). Also controls \(\beta_1\), \(A_{s,min}\), and \(E_c = 4700\sqrt{f'_c}\).
fy MPa
Steel yield strength (420 = Grade 60)
fy is the yield stress. Grade 60 = 420 MPa. Design: \(A_s = \frac{M_u}{\phi f_y (d - a/2)}\). Higher fy = less steel, but too high may cause compression-controlled behavior.
SECTION B — Section Geometry
h mm
Total section depth
h = total depth. Effective depth \(d = h - cover\). Capacity \(M_n = A_s f_y(d-a/2)\). ACI min: simply supported \(L/16\), continuous \(L/21\).
b_web mm
Web width (slabs: 1000 mm strip)
b_web = web width. For slabs: 1000mm. Used in min steel: \(A_{s,min} = \max\left(\frac{0.25\sqrt{f'_c}}{f_y}b_w d,\;\frac{1.4 b_w d}{f_y}\right)\).
b_eff mm
Effective flange width (= b_web for rect.)
b_eff = effective compression width. For T-beams: \(b_{eff} \le \min(L/4, b_w+16h_f)\). Used in \(a = \frac{A_s f_y}{0.85 f'_c b_{eff}}\).
Cover mm
Cover to centroid of tension steel
Cover to bar centroid. \(d = h - cover\). Includes clear cover + tie + half bar. Typical: slabs 30–40, beams 50–65 mm.
SECTION C — Factored Load
Mu kN.m
Ultimate factored moment
Mu = factored moment. \(M_u = 1.2M_D + 1.6M_L\). Design: \(\phi M_n \ge M_u\). Multiply by \(10^6\) to convert kN.m to N.mm.
SECTION D — Design Assumptions
j (estimate) —
j is initial lever arm ratio. Only for first iteration. Final result uses actual \((d-a/2)\).
