SECTION A — Material Properties
f'c MPa
Concrete compressive strength
f'c is the 28-day cylinder compressive strength. For columns: \(P_0 = 0.85f'_c(A_g-A_{st})+f_yA_{st}\), stress block \(a = \beta_1 c\), and \(\beta_1\) factor.
fy MPa
Steel yield strength
fy for longitudinal bars. Steel works in tension AND compression in columns. \(E_s = 200{,}000\) MPa.
SECTION B — Column Geometry
h (depth) mm
Column depth (in bending direction)
h = depth in bending direction. \(A_g = b \times h\). Effective depth \(d = h - d'\).
b (width) mm
Column width (perpendicular to bending)
b = width. Compression block: \(C_c = 0.85 f'_c \cdot a \cdot b\).
Cover (d') mm
Distance to bar centroid from nearest face
d' = cover to bar centroid. Steel layer distance: \(h - 2d'\). Typical: 40-65 mm.
SECTION C — Reinforcement
Ast (total) mm2
Total steel area (all bars)
Ast = total steel. ACI: \(0.01A_g \le A_{st} \le 0.08A_g\). Ratio \(\rho = A_{st}/A_g\).
Layout
Bar distribution pattern
2-face: best for uniaxial. 4-face: for biaxial. 4-face diagram is smaller (bars near neutral axis).
SECTION D — Applied Loads
Pu kN
Factored axial load
Pu factored axial load. Must plot inside interaction diagram. Max: \(\phi \cdot 0.80 P_0\).
Mu kN.m
Factored moment
Mu factored moment. Eccentricity \(e=M_u/P_u\). Balanced point: max Mn where concrete crushes and steel yields simultaneously.
